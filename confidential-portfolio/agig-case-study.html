Strategic Analysis & Risk Management Approach

Case Study: Discovery-First vs. Implementation-First Methodologies

Australian Gas Infrastructure Group (AGIG)

Multi-Domain Active Directory Consolidation Project

Project Context & Business Environment

Organizational Structure

  • Acquisition-Based Growth: Multiple business units operating as separate entities
  • Geographic Distribution: Gas operations across multiple Australian states
  • Legacy Infrastructure: 3-4 Active Directory domains from acquired companies
  • Operational Complexity: Separate budgeting, financing, and operational procedures

Perceived Problem Statement

Leadership Directive: "Multiple AD domains create management and operational overhead - consolidate them"

Critical Gap: No clear articulation of underlying business problem or strategic objective

"Consolidation for consolidation's sake doesn't add business value when current structure supports how the business actually operates"

Project Challenges & Constraints

High-Risk Factors

  • Vendor Lock-In: 100% vendor-operated environment
  • Limited Access: Read-only permissions to existing infrastructure
  • Knowledge Gaps: No single person understands holistic business operations
  • Legacy Dependencies: Unknown application integrations with AD features

Strategic Considerations

  • Regulatory Environment: Energy sector compliance requirements
  • Operational Continuity: 24/7 critical infrastructure operations
  • Business Alignment: Current structure supports operational model
  • Industry Precedent: Peers maintain hybrid architectures

Core Issues Identified

  1. Undefined Problem: Leadership hasn't clearly articulated what business problem consolidation solves
  2. Scope Uncertainty: Unknown dependencies and integration complexities
  3. Vendor Risk: Authorizing undefined scope of work with unlimited budget exposure
  4. Strategic Misalignment: Technical solution may not address actual business needs

Strategic vs. Tactical Approaches

Strategic Approach (My Recommendation)

Core Philosophy: Question the premise - understand the problem before solving it

Key Principles:

  • Discovery-first methodology
  • Root cause analysis
  • Risk-based decision making
  • Business alignment validation

Primary Question:

"Should we consolidate these domains?"

Tactical Approach (Colleague's Options)

Core Philosophy: Accept the requirement - focus on execution methods

Key Focus Areas:

  • Implementation options analysis
  • Technical migration strategies
  • Timeline and resource planning
  • Technology selection

Primary Question:

"How should we consolidate these domains?"
Fundamental Disconnect: Operating at different levels - Strategic vs. Tactical

Discovery-First "Mini-Project" Approach

Strategic Rationale

Transform undefined, high-risk transformation into bounded, low-risk assessment project

Phase 1: Comprehensive Discovery (3-4 months)

Milestone 1.1: Application Dependency Mapping
  • Catalog all applications with AD authentication dependencies
  • Identify SCADA, industrial control systems, and legacy applications
  • Map authentication protocols (Kerberos, NTLM, LDAP)
  • Gate: Complete application inventory with risk classification
Milestone 1.2: Business Impact Assessment
  • Assess business criticality of each application
  • Calculate cost of application remediation vs. hybrid maintenance
  • Evaluate regulatory and compliance implications
  • Gate: Business case validation for each consolidation option
Milestone 1.3: Strategic Options Analysis
  • Evaluate AD consolidation vs. hybrid architecture vs. status quo
  • Assess cloud migration feasibility and timeline
  • Compare costs, risks, and benefits of each approach
  • Gate: Executive decision on strategic direction

Risk Mitigation & Strategic Benefits

Discovery-First Benefits

  • Defined Scope: Bounded project with clear deliverables
  • Cost Control: Fixed budget for assessment vs. open-ended implementation
  • Risk Reduction: Identify surprises before committing resources
  • Informed Decisions: Data-driven strategic choices
  • Vendor Accountability: Demonstrate competency before major engagement
  • Executive Comfort: Easier to approve "study" than transformation

Implementation-First Risks

  • Scope Creep: Every surprise becomes expensive change order
  • Cost Blowouts: Unknown dependencies = unlimited budget exposure
  • Business Disruption: Critical systems failure during migration
  • Vendor Lock-In: Reactive position with limited negotiating power
  • Wrong Solution: Solving undefined problem may not add value
  • Career Risk: "Explain the uncosted approach you selected"
"We shouldn't authorize a vendor to perform surgery when we haven't done the diagnostic workup first"

Industry Reality: 75-80% of Australian gas utilities maintain hybrid AD architectures long-term

Australian Energy Sector Reality

Why Gas Utilities Stay Hybrid

  • Regulatory Requirements: National Gas Rules, state safety regulations
  • Legacy Industrial Systems: SCADA, HMI systems with Windows authentication
  • Operational Continuity: 24/7 critical infrastructure cannot tolerate authentication failures
  • Geographic Complexity: Remote facilities with limited connectivity
  • Vendor Dependencies: Industrial applications with no modern alternatives

Migration Challenges Specific to Energy Sector

  • Legacy Applications: 10-20 year old systems with deep AD integration
  • Knowledge Loss: Original developers and system architects no longer available
  • Safety Systems: Cannot test authentication changes on live safety instrumented systems
  • Compliance Audit Trails: Historical access logs cannot be migrated

Strategic Recommendation

Accept hybrid architecture as long-term operational model rather than viewing it as transition state

Focus modernization on new applications while maintaining stability for legacy systems
